old models

The Hofmeister kink fulfills at least two important design functions: On the one hand, it works well as a historical design link between BMWs of different generations, past, present, and future. And, it is a design element which helps the eye make the transition from the greenhouse area (that is, the windows, roof, and pillars) to the lower body of the car.

But, where does the name Hofmeister kink come from? It dates back to former BMW Director of Body Engineering and Development Wilhelm Hofmeister. Incidentally, he was not a designer, but rather an engineer and a division manager. And, at that time, the division manager for body development was also the de facto head of BMW Design. Moreover, according to the BMW design expert Carp, one must admit: the distinctive kink is not a BMW invention, nor did it come from Hofmeister. It had already made its appearance on vehicle bodies in the 1940s, in automobiles from the USA and Italy. First in two-door coupés and even convertibles, and subsequently in saloons as well. In the mid 1950s, there were already a few drawings showing a kink in the C-pillar. From 1960 on, there were serious proposals for it. According to Carp, the influence of the Italian designers Giovanni Michelotti and Giorgio Giugiaro (➜ Read also: How to create a classic) ensured that the element found its start in the design language of BMW and, thanks to Hofmeister, that it remained.

80-90s models

Production Years: 1991-1999 Key Features: The E36 was known for its refined handling and improved build quality over its predecessor. It introduced new technology like a multi-link rear suspension and was available in sedan, coupe, convertible, and touring (wagon) versions.

BMW 5 Series (E34) Production Years: 1988-1996 Key Features: The E34 was praised for its spacious interior, solid build, and balanced handling. It was a major step forward in terms of comfort and technology. 525i/530i/540i: Offered a range of engine options, from more economical to high-performance V8s. M5 (E34): Featured a 3.8L inline-six engine, known for its impressive performance.

2000s models

Production Years: 1994-2001 Key Features: The E38 was BMW's flagship sedan, offering advanced technology and luxury. It introduced features like GPS navigation and adaptive headlights. 728i/730i/740i: Offered a range of engines, including a powerful V8 in the 740i. 750iL: Equipped with a 5.4L V12 engine, representing the pinnacle of BMW luxury at the time.

2010s models

BMW 3 Series (F30/F31/F34) Production Years: 2011-2019 Key Features: The F30 series brought improvements in technology and comfort compared to the E90. It featured enhanced engines, better fuel efficiency, and more advanced infotainment systems. Notable Variants: 320i/328i/335i: Offered a range of engines including turbocharged options. M3 (F80): Known for its high-performance 3.0L inline-six engine with turbocharging and advanced handling.

2020s models

BMW iX Production Years: 2021-present Key Features: All-electric SUV known for its high-tech features and spacious interior. Notable Features: Modern design, advanced driver assistance features, and a long electric range. BMW 7 Series (G70) Production Years: 2022-present Key Features: The latest generation of BMW's flagship sedan, featuring advanced technology and luxury. Notable Variants: 740i/750i: Includes advanced engines and features. i7: The all-electric version of the 7 Series, offering cutting-edge technology and performance. Key Trends and Technologies Electrification: The 2020s have seen a significant push towards electric and hybrid models, with the i4 and iX representing BMWs commitment to electric mobility. Advanced Technology: Both decades have featured advancements in driver assistance systems, infotainment, and connectivity, but the 2020s models have pushed these technologies even further with features like advanced autonomous driving capabilities.
